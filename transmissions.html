Transmission Flushes- Do you really need it? This will help you decide

  • by Admin
  • 3 min read
  • 20 Comment

Did you know that in 2015, almost 55% of Americans didn't use all their vacation time? This is according to a study by the US Travel Association's Project Time Off. A lot of the days that weren't used were days that could not be rolled over or exchanged for money. "Project Time Off" also found that there was a correlation between planning vacations and more happiness. Working endlessly isn't good for anyone. You need to take a break to keep from losing your mind and for better health. I am not talking about the vacation you take where your boss and your co workers still call, email and text you about work. I am talking about a real unplugged vacation. Taking vacations significantly reduces stress which is good for you mentally and good for your relationships. Taking a vacation has many benefits. Many studies show that people feel more refreshed, positive and mentally clear after a vacation. Some studies even show that vacations can reduce heart disease and reduce depression and anxiety. Vacations, even short ones, also make you more productive. Planning and preparing and sometimes even the vacation itself can be stressful. So let’s not defeat the purpose of taking a vacation by stressing out. Follow these tips to stay cool and calm.

  • 1. Plan,plan, plan
    You may not be able to plan everything, but most things you can plan in advance. Choose your dates and plan your schedule. Having a schedule helps to make things less stressful.
  • 2. Start early
    Most airlines let you book at least 6 months in advance. Book as far out as you can to save yourself the stress of watching fares and wondering if the price today is the lowest or wether or not you should wait.
  • 3. Buy insurance
    Anything that can go wrong, will. So don't skimp on the insurance. Protect your investment in case someone get's ill or other unforseen circumstances.
  • 4. Don’t sweat the small things.
    Things won't go right sometimes. Don't sweat it. Have a backup plan and move on.
  • 5.Purchase guided tours
    Having someone knowledgeable taking car of all the details of transportation, locations, etc. takes a lot of the strain off of you. Get a travel agent. They are starting to become popular. Just tell them what you want, and they do all the research and bookings for you. Less stress.
