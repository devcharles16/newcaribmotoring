Transmission Flushes - Do you really need them?

  • by Admin
  • 3 min read

At some point or another, your mechanic has probably suggested you do a transmission flush. Is it really necessary though? It is usually recommended by your vehicles manufacturer on a schedule. Refer to your vehicle's owner's manual to check the recommendations. Everyone wishes they'd done one regularly as soon as they start to experience issues with their transmission.
What is a transmission flush? A transmission flush is a process where all of the transmission oil is removed. A machine is used to force out all the dirt and debris then refills the transmission with new oil. This should not be confused with a drain and fill which still leaves old transmission oil within the transmission itself.

Here's when you should have one done:

  1. If your vehicle manufacturer recommends it at specific intervals

  2. If your transmission fluid is burnt

  3. Problems shifting gears

  4. Slipping gears

  5. The car is surging forward


It is important however, to take your vehicle to a certified mechanic to make sure there are no existing transmission issues. Some of the problems listed can be caused by other transmission issues.

How much does a transmission flush cost?
A transmission flush could cost you anywhere from $100-$400. It all depends on the type of vehicle you have and your location. Most people pay between $150 to $200. If you are looking for something more cost effective, there are also commercial products available for cleaning your transmission lines.
  • Lubegard 19001 Dr. Tranny Kooler Kleen Trasmission Line Flush
  • Liqui Moly Pro-Line Automatic Transmission Cleaner

A lot of mechanics do not recommend transmission flushes on older vehicles because so many people swear that their transmissions failed after a fluid flush. Large peices of gunk and debris can from the transmission can get into the valve body and cause transmission problems. Whatever you decided, it is always a good idea to have your vehicle diagnosed by a professional.
