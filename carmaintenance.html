5 Maintenance Tips to keep your vehicle running smooth

  • by Admin
  • 3 min read

Having a healthy, well maintained car is only possible with a well taken care of engine. Your engine needs to run smoothly to keep your vehicle in tip-top shape. Do you know the basic things that need to be done to maintain your engine? What kind of expenses can arise if your engine is not maintained correctly? A properly maintained engine can save you money, improve your car’s performance, and make it last longer.
Here are 5 engine maintenance tips that’ll help your engine run like new and extend the life of your car.

  1. Change your oil regularly
    Engine oil lubricates engine parts and reduces friction to keep parts from wearing down. Most oils also have additives that help break down debris. It traps all the dust and dirt, so they don't get into other parts of the car. Over time, the oil loses its ability to perform these functions. That is why oil changes are so significant. Oil grade and change times are subject to the manufacturer’s recommendations. Check your owner’s manual to verify how often you should change the oil.
  2. Change your air filter
    I know a lot of people turn down the recommendation for air filter changes when technicians try to upsell them. The truth is, although they are upselling you, it is still a necessity. Changing your car's air filter regularly extends your engine life because the air filter traps dirt and debris that can damage engine parts. Each vehicle manufacturer had different recommendations for how often air filters should be replaced. As always, check your owner’s manual for your vehicle's recommendations. Your mechanic can also recommend when to change it depending on where you live or your driving habits. More frequent driving and driving in dusty areas usually require more frequent air filter changes.
  3. Don't ignore your check engine light
    Manufacturers put it there for a reason. I can't tell you how many times people come to my shop and when I ask how long the check engine light has been on, the answer is usually weeks to months. Yes, sometimes it comes on for something minor like a bad sensor or a loose gas cap. However, it could also be something little that could become major like a failing thermostat. A thermostat that is stuck open allows the coolant to continuously circulate and causes the engine to take longer to get to it's operating temperature. This can affect your engine's life. A closed thermostat is a major problem because if the coolant can't circulate, the engine's elements build up heat and can cause severe damage.
  4. Replace your fuel filter
    The fuel filter filters out junk from the fuel so that nothing enters the combustion chamber. A new clean filter allows a free flow of clean fuel to the fuel pump and engine. This guarantees that there is less build-up inside the engine, keeping it running with optimum performance.
  5. Check for leaks
    Take care of any leaks ASAP. If you are not paying attention and all the oil runs out, say goodbye to your engine. Your engine needs oil to run and without it, it will die. Fix all leaks to avoid this problem.
Following these tips should keep your engine running smoothly and reduce the need for costly repairs. Replacing an engine is not cheap, and replacing it could leave you without a car for weeks. So take care of it.

4 Tire Maintenance Tips to Extend Your Tire's Life

  • by Admin
  • 3 min read

Tire maintenance is a vital part of keeping your car running smoothly. Under-inflated tires lead to premature wear, tread separation, and blowouts, which can be dangerous. They can also reduce your vehicle’s gas mileage.
Follow these 5 tips to keep your tires in tip-top shape.

  1. Check Your Tire Pressure Regularly
    Tires can lose approximately one pound per square inch of air (PSI) per month. In cold weather, they could lose more. Sometimes a visual inspection can tell if your tires are underinflated. Still, you probably won't notice a slight difference in the air pressure with a visual inspection. You should check your tire pressure at least once a month. It's super easy to check using a tire pressure guage . You can find the proper tire pressures for your car in your car's owner's manual or the placard on your car's door frame.
  2. Rotate Your Tires
    A tire rotation is when you change the position of each tire on your vehicle. This should happen about every 5000- 6000 miles or so. Always check your vehicle's manual for your manufacturer's recommendation on frequency and rotation patterns (forward/rearward cross, x-pattern, front to back, or side to side). You need to regularly rotate your tires to help promote even wear on each tire, which maximizes your tire life.
  3. Keep Your Wheels Aligned
    A tire or wheel alignment is necessary for proper tire performance and to help your tires last longer. When your wheels are out of alignment, they affect your vehicle handling and cause pulling in one direction or vibrations. This can cause uneven wear on your tires, causing them to need to be replaced a lot sooner.
  4. Perform Regular Visual Inspections
    Tires wear.Tires wear. It's just a natural part of owning a vehicle. Performing a visual inspection on your tires regularly can save you from having an accident on the road or being stranded with a flat tire. You can check your tire tread using a tire tread gauge , or you can use a penny. Place a penny into the tread in different parts of the tire. If you see the top of lincoln's head most of the time, then it's time to replace the tire. Also, look for signs of severe wear and visible steel wires.

Following these tips will help keep you safe and save you money in gas and expensive repairs if you have an accident. So, it's important to check your tires regularly.
